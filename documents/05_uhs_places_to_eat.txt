A campus guide to places to eat at UC Berkeley.

Crossroads Dining Common. Described as the heart of the dining commons, convenient for Units 1, 2, and 3 and close to campus. Serves breakfast, lunch, dinner, and a popular Late Night option after 10 pm.

Cafe 3. Located inside Unit 3 next to the Bear Market. Smaller than Crossroads with similar food and a very nice relaxed environment featuring comfy seats and colorful sofas.

Bear Market. A mini food mart in Unit 3 stocked with ramen, drinks, chips, candies, and Ben & Jerry's ice cream.

The Den / Peet's Coffee. Next to Crossroads. Sells pastries, sandwiches, and smoothies — a good morning pick-me-up spot.

Golden Bear Cafe. Near Sather Gate on campus. A popular spot offering smoothies, wraps, salads, and more — like a larger Bear Market.

Foothill Dining Common. Near the Foothill dorms. Has late night and is famous for its gigantic steaks.

Clark Kerr Cafeteria. Clark Kerr's dining common, a fun change of pace from Crossroads with very delicious food if you are willing to walk the distance for it.

Pat Brown's. On the north side of campus. Good breakfast selection plus Peet's Coffee — recommended for early morning classes.

Qualcomm Cyber Cafe. Offers awesome wraps and Peet's Coffee.

Common Grounds. On Floor F of Dwinelle. Serves Peet's Coffee and a wide selection of teas, described as a great, secret location.

Terrace Cafe. Offers grab-and-go sandwiches, sushi, and coffee.

Ramona's. Near Bancroft and College. Has drinks, snacks, dim sum, and sandwiches, plus outdoor seating.

The View Cafe. Near Lawrence Hall of Science. A bit far but offers lunch options and a great view of the bay.
