A student writer ranks all four UC Berkeley dining halls.

Crossroads (ranked #1). The writer admits hearing a lot of "Croads slander" but still ranks it first. They praise its consistency, noting that every dish is pretty good on most days. As a Southside dorm freshman they find it very convenient. They highlight unique items found only at Crossroads, including Korean Fried Chicken and waffles from the waffle maker.

Foothill (ranked #2). The writer docks points for distance, noting Foothill is quite far from the southside of campus. However, they appreciate the pre-made wrapped sandwiches you can take to go, which other halls lack — handy when you're in a rush. Overall they found the food pretty good and consistent.

Cafe 3 (ranked #3). Described as very convenient, but the writer criticizes its limited options and says it is often difficult for those with food allergies to get meals. They mention long lines making seconds hard to get, sometimes leaving with their stomach only half-full.

Clark Kerr (ranked #4). The writer offers a hot take against the common opinion that Clark Kerr has the best food. They note it is the farthest hall (a convenience point off) and serves fried foods daily, which they avoid while trying to eat healthier. They also criticize inaccurate menus — sometimes being served a different dish than advertised. Still, they concede the dishes are not bad at all.

Popular menu items mentioned across the halls include orange chicken, mac and cheese, pulled pork sandwiches, fettuccine alfredo, and tri-tip, plus desserts like red velvet cake and chocolate.
