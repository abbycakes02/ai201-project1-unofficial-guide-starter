Meal plan structure. Meal plans through Cal Dining allow access to the four dining halls on campus. There is the Blue meal plan, which gives 12 meals per week and 500 flex dollars. The Gold meal plan provides unlimited meal swipes per week and 750 flex dollars. Current students with residence hall contracts follow a slightly different structure — students with a Residence Hall contract are automatically enrolled in the Blue & Gold Plan, which includes meal swipes and flex dollars.

Crossroads, fall 2023 first-year perspective. Crossroads, also known as "Croads" in the Berkeley community, is one of the four dining halls at UC Berkeley. In fall 2023 they added an Asian bar which serves foods such as pho, dumplings, and Thai curry. The reviewer enjoys eating at Crossroads as the meals are mostly nutritional and flavorful. It is the closest dining hall to residences of Unit 1 and Unit 2, but due to its popularity, people from other residence halls come to Croads, leading to lines for entering the building and for the food. The food varies from day to day, but during special events they had ribeye steak, crab legs (seafood boil), Korean chicken tenders, and even boba.

Crossroads as flagship. Crossroads is consistently described as the flagship dining hall, noted for its international cuisine and contemporary feel. Its 10 stations include pizza, deli, pho, and a salad bar, with food labeling, allergen-free platforms, and attention to dietary demands.
