Foothill dining hall. Students consistently praise the food quality but criticize the location. As far as location, Foothill is gorgeous with YMCA vibes, and the dining hall is objectively the best — however, it is a TREK to get where you need to go. Reviews are mixed: one student noted the onsite dining hall received mixed reviews, but the beautiful views and serene atmosphere won over many residents. On convenience, Foothill is quite far from the southside of campus, so that is a point off. However, even if you dine in, they have pre-made sandwiches that are wrapped and that you can take to go, which other dining halls don't have. The big drawback is the uphill walk: with its location north of campus, the walk up to Foothill can be a lot at the beginning of the year, especially because most of it is uphill.

Clark Kerr dining hall. Clark Kerr is known for its distinctive atmosphere. The dining hall exudes a mission-esque vibe, fostering a pleasant ambiance for meals. Like Foothill, it is far and uphill: situated in the serene Berkeley hills, this dorm provides a secluded and peaceful environment, though the uphill trek to campus guarantees a good workout.

Comparison context. Student consensus across blogs and review sites tends to rank Foothill among the best for food quality (despite the uphill hike), while Clark Kerr is appreciated more for its unique mission-style ambiance than its menu. The trade-off for both is location — both require a significant uphill walk from main campus.
