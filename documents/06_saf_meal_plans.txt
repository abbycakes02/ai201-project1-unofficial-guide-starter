A guide to using meal plans at UC Berkeley.

Overview. Berkeley's dining system simplifies meals for students living on campus, with services available when classes are in session.

Meal swipes. A set number of weekly credits redeemable at the dining commons or as meal-equivalent value at spots like Browns and the Golden Bear Cafe. Unused swipes reset weekly at midnight, and must be used during one of three windows: breakfast, lunch, or dinner.

Flex dollars. These work like cash with a diminishing balance, allotted per semester.

The writer praised the buffet-style options at Crossroads, a three-minute walk from the dorm, noting you can eat as much food as you want.

The four dining commons. Cafe 3 is a casual lounge with two dining stations, a kosher station, and the Bear Market quick-service store; it offers vegan, vegetarian, kosher, and allergen-friendly options. Clark Kerr is an ambient space with four hot meal stations, a deli bar, and a salad bar, including halal, vegetarian, allergy-free, and gluten-free choices. Foothill serves Indian, Asian, and American cuisine across 12 stations, including halal options and the allergy-free Bear Fit Station. Crossroads is Berkeley's flagship eatery known for international cuisine, with 10 stations and dietary and allergen accommodations.

Meal plan options. The Advantage Plan offers 5 weekly swipes plus $250 flex dollars per semester. The Blue Plan offers 12 weekly swipes plus $300 flex dollars per semester. The Gold Plan offers 10 weekly swipes plus $500 flex dollars per semester. The Ultimate Plan offers unlimited swipes (one every 30 minutes per meal hour) plus $500 flex dollars per semester.
