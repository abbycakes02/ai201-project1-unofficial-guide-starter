A guide to eating on campus at UC Berkeley with a meal plan.

Meal plans. Meal plans come bundled with on-campus housing contracts, and students can pay to upgrade. The author chose the Ultimate Plan, which offered unlimited swipes plus flex dollars.

Dining halls (Cafe 3, Clark Kerr, Crossroads, Foothill). Each hall has its own vibe — Clark Kerr is described as having a Hogwarts-esque vibe with a similarly magical menu, Crossroads is modern with varied cuisine stations, while Cafe 3 and Foothill feel homier and casual. Menus rotate every meal. Author favorites: orange chicken, mac and cheese, pulled pork sandwiches, fettuccine alfredo, and tri-tip, plus desserts like red velvet cake and chocolate chip cookies.

Golden Bear Cafe. Located in Sproul Plaza, good for a quick lunch or coffee, with grab-and-go options purchasable via flex dollars. Ordering is easy with short waits. Favorites: Philly Cheesesteak, Chicken Tenders, and Bulgogi Fries. Has a Peet's Coffee, the author's go-to for iced lattes.

Brown's. In the northwest corner of campus, focused on healthy California cuisine with sustainable and organic meals. Popular items: salmon filet, garlic fries, and tri-tip, with sauces like chimichurri and ancho orange.

MLK Student Union eateries (first floor). Monsoon Kitchens serves Indian food — katora bowls with chicken tikka masala or palak paneer, plus samosas and naan. Ladle & Leaf serves salads, soups, and sandwiches; the author's usual is a turkey pesto sandwich or chicken chipotle salad with turkey chili or New England clam chowder.

Local x Design. UC Berkeley's newest location, in an area that previously lacked accessible food options. Currently accepts flex dollars only. Recommended: the Boichik Bagel sandwich for breakfast, or a salad, protein bowl, or sandwich for lunch.

Overall, the author recommends eating with a meal plan as a convenient and delicious experience without the hassle of cooking.
