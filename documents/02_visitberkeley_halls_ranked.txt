A second student writer ranks the four dining halls — and disagrees sharply with other rankings.

Cafe 3 (ranked #4). Located on the south side in the Unit 3 courtyard. Known for vegetarian and vegan options, though the writer notes all halls offer similar plant-based choices. The interior feels dated with a hodgepodge of neon colors reminiscent of decades past, but there is ample indoor and outdoor seating. Considered a safe, convenient choice that serves two of the largest residence halls.

Foothill (ranked #3). Described as a cabin-like oasis with a breathtaking view of the campanile and eastern campus. The downside is the steep hike to reach it. The cozy lodge vibe is consistent, but the food is sometimes a hit or miss — occasionally she had to settle for a simple slice of pizza. A convenience store is attached, offering snacks for flex dollars.

Crossroads (ranked #2). Offers the most classic dining hall experience with options rivaled by none. Located between Units 1 and 2, it gets the heaviest foot traffic. A favorite feature is the BearFit build-your-own-bowl bar (she chose sweet potatoes, kale, chickpeas). Notable for having a deep-fryer, with truffle and garlic fries as her favorite. The main flaw is the depressing brutalist metal and cement look.

Clark Kerr (ranked #1). Ranked first largely for ambiance — it serves only breakfast and dinner, and at dinner is filled with a beautiful golden glow. Despite being small, Clark Kerr always seems to have the best food, from steak to biscuits. The downside is the long 15 to 20 minute walk, but she insists it is worth the trek, calling not visiting more often one of her biggest freshman-year regrets.
