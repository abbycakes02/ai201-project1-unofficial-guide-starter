A UC Berkeley student reviews several dining halls and on-campus spots.

Crossroads. The reviewer enjoys this hall, noting the meals are mostly nutritious and tasty. An Asian bar was added in fall 2023 serving pho, dumplings, and Thai curry. It is the closest hall to Units 1 and 2, but its popularity causes lines — both for entering the building and for the food. Special events featured ribeye steak, crab legs (a seafood boil), Korean chicken tenders, and boba. As described, typically there is some source of protein, a grill, a vegetarian option, and a salad bar.

Cafe 3. Rated poorly: Cafe 3 is known to be one of the worst dining halls at UC Berkeley. The reviewer finds the food either very salty or very bland and carries hot sauce to compensate. One upside — there is rarely a line since it is unpopular.

Foothill. Praised for those living in Foothill or on Northside. The reviewer enjoyed the Korean bibimbap, saying it was very similar to what their mom would have made back home. Their main complaint is that it is not spicy enough.

Browns (a restaurant that accepts meal swipes). The reviewer's favorite on-campus spot. Their lunch order is rotisserie tri-tip with Szechuan sauce, garlic fries, and salad. They claim these are the best fries that UC Berkeley offers in any restaurant. Avocado toast is available in the mornings.

Golden Bear Cafe (GBC). A community staple — some off-campus students get the 5-day plan just for it. The reviewer likes the Buffalo Chicken Salad, or fries when indulging.

Ladle & Leaf. Good for cutting weight; the reviewer ate Chicken Chipotle twice daily for a week. Nutrient-packed with good protein, though it may not be the most filling.

Monsoon Kitchen. Recommended for Indian food lovers, with vegetarian options.
