Each dining hall has its own distinct character and layout. Clark Kerr has a Hogwarts-esque vibe with a similarly magical menu. Crossroads is more modern with many stations offering a variety of cuisines, while Cafe 3 and Foothill emit a more homey and casual feel. A perk for variety-seekers: the dining halls change up their menus for every meal, which is good for those who get bored of the same foods every day.

On Clark Kerr specifically, a common complaint is the wait: the line is often very long, so it is hard to get seconds, and sometimes students leave with their stomach only half-full. Clark Kerr is often reputed as having the best food, but its far location works against it.

On Crossroads, students rave about special events like seafood boils, and praise unique offerings such as Korean Fried Chicken and waffles from the waffle maker that are only available there.

On Cafe 3, the recurring criticism is that it doesn't have as many options as the other dining halls, and that it is often difficult for those with food allergies to get meals.

On Foothill, the standout features are the pre-made wrapped grab-and-go sandwiches and, per some students, gigantic steaks — balanced against the long uphill walk to get there.
